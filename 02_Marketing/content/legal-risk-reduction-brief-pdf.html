The Challenge

AI-generated video content creates three exposure categories for brands:

  1. Copyright infringement risk — training data lawsuits, output similarity claims
  2. Regulatory compliance risk — FTC disclosure rules, state AI laws, platform violations
  3. Copyrightability uncertainty — can your brand actually own the AI-generated commercial?

Traditional stock libraries and AI tool providers address provenance (WHERE content came from) but not legal defensibility (whether you can USE it).

3. Copyrightability Protection (Ownership Certainty)

U.S. Copyright Office Ruling (January 2025):

"Given current generally available technology, prompts alone do not provide sufficient human control to make users of an AI system the authors of the output... However, [in cases of substantial modification or expressive input] the human's own creative expression will be protected by copyright."

What this means: If your brand commissions AI-generated content without documented human authorship, you may not own the copyright to your own commercial.

SI8 solution:

  • Human Authorship Declaration required: 150+ word documentation of editorial choices, iteration decisions, post-generation modifications

Without this documentation, your brand cannot register copyright or enforce IP rights.
