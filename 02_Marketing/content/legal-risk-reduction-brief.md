## The Challenge

AI-generated video content creates three exposure categories for brands:

1. **Copyright infringement risk** — training data lawsuits, output similarity claims
2. **Regulatory compliance risk** — FTC disclosure rules, state AI laws, platform violations
3. **Copyrightability uncertainty** — can your brand actually own the AI-generated commercial?

Traditional stock libraries and AI tool providers address provenance (WHERE content came from) but not legal defensibility (whether you can USE it).

### 1. Damage Mitigation (73%+ Statutory Reduction)

**Legal Framework:** U.S. copyright law provides reduced statutory damages for "innocent infringement" when defendants demonstrate good-faith belief they were not infringing.

**Statutory Damages:**
- Standard: $750 - $30,000 per work
- **With documented good faith: $200 - $750 per work** (73% minimum reduction)
- Willful infringement: up to $150,000 per work

**Requirement:** *"Considerable effort must go into establishing lack of willful disregard, often requiring detailed documentation and compelling argumentation."* (Copyright Alliance)

**How SI8's Rights Package supports this:**
- **Field #1 (Tool Provenance Log):** Documents which AI tools were used, versions, paid commercial plan receipts
- **Field #3 (Safe Lane Sign-off):** SI8's legal review creates "compelling argumentation" courts require
- **Field #4 (Commercial Use Authorization):** Demonstrates good-faith reliance on tool provider terms
- **Field #9 (Version History):** Creates audit trail showing reasonable care

**Source:** 17 U.S.C. § 504(c)(2)

**What this means:** Colorado's AI law explicitly rewards documented systems. SI8's Rights Package = statutory compliance documentation.

### 3. Copyrightability Protection (Ownership Certainty)

**U.S. Copyright Office Ruling (January 2025):**

> "Given current generally available technology, prompts alone do not provide sufficient human control to make users of an AI system the authors of the output... However, [in cases of substantial modification or expressive input] the human's own creative expression will be protected by copyright."

**What this means:** If your brand commissions AI-generated content without documented human authorship, **you may not own the copyright** to your own commercial.

**SI8 solution:**
- **Human Authorship Declaration required:** 150+ word documentation of editorial choices, iteration decisions, post-generation modifications
- **Field #9 (Version History):** Captures the editorial paper trail USCO requires for copyrightability

**Without this documentation, your brand cannot register copyright or enforce IP rights.**

**Source:** U.S. Copyright Office Part 2 Report (Jan 2025)
