## LinkedIn Post — Draft A (C2PA Gap angle — most differentiated)

In January, the IAB released its first-ever AI Transparency and Disclosure Framework.

It requires brands and agencies to do four things before running AI-generated video in campaigns:

1. Assess whether the content could mislead consumers
2. Apply standardized disclosure labels
3. Implement C2PA metadata
4. Document the AI tools used in production

Most agencies are focused on #3 — C2PA metadata. Embed the technical provenance data in the file. Done.

Here's the problem.

C2PA tells you what tool made the video. It does not tell you whether the content is IP-clean, whether any real person's likeness was used without authorization, whether the training data is cleared for commercial use, or whether the content meets brand safety standards across your client's categories.

That assessment is not automated. It requires human judgment.

The IAB framework creates the requirement. The question is what actually satisfies it.

Chain of Title documentation — tool provenance, authorship declaration, likeness assessment, IP review, brand safety, and a risk rating — is the record that makes the assessment defensible.

The framework is 10 weeks old. Most agencies haven't caught up yet.
