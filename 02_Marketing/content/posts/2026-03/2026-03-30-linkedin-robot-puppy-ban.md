## Draft

Facebook just banned an AI-generated ad for misleading customers.

The ad showed a robot puppy. Customers thought they were buying a real one.

The brand didn't intend to deceive anyone — they just deployed AI video without documenting what it was and what it claimed to show.

That's the risk brands and agencies are taking every week with unverified AI content.

No paper trail. No disclosure record. No way to prove you knew what was in the video before you ran it.

A banned ad is the mild outcome. The worse version involves a complaint to an advertising standards body, a regulatory fine, or a lawsuit from a customer who made a purchase decision based on misleading AI content.

Chain of Title documentation doesn't just protect you from copyright issues. It creates the audit trail that shows what your video is, what tools made it, and what was disclosed — before it ran.

That's what separates "we ran AI content" from "we ran vetted, documented AI content."
