## Draft

OpenAI just shut down Sora.

Disney pulled a $1 billion investment.

If you built your AI video workflow around a single vendor, you just lost it.

This is the second time in the past year a major AI video tool has disappeared or pivoted away from commercial availability. It won't be the last.

The agencies and brands that are fine right now have one thing in common: they didn't tie their compliance documentation to a specific tool. They documented the content — what it is, how it was made, what rights exist — independent of which platform generated it.

Chain of Title documentation is tool-agnostic by design. Sora shuts down. Runway pivots. Kling changes its terms. The documentation stays valid.

We removed Sora from our verified tools list last week. Our clients' existing Sora footage still has valid Chain of Title. Nothing changes for them.

That's the point.
