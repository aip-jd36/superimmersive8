## The Problem: AI Video Rights Are Messy

Brands are using AI-generated video in campaigns. Most content comes from social feeds with **zero commercial rights documentation**.

**The risks are real:**
- **Training Data Lawsuits:** Major studios (Disney, Warner Bros) are suing AI video platforms for training on copyrighted content without permission (Minimax/Hailuo, Nov 2025). Users who license outputs from these platforms can be held liable.
- **Likeness & Voice Cloning:** Using real people's faces or voices without consent violates publicity rights laws in most jurisdictions. New AI-specific legislation in California, New York, and Tennessee creates additional exposure.
- **Platform Crackdowns:** YouTube, TikTok, and Meta are enforcing AI disclosure requirements. Undocumented content risks demonetization, removal, or account bans. TikTok increased AI content removals by 340% in 2025.
- **Copyright Uncertainty:** AI-generated content produced solely through prompt entry is **not copyrightable** in the US, Taiwan, or Singapore. Without human creative direction, there's no IP to license.
- **Tool Terms Violations:** Most AI tools prohibit commercial use on free/consumer tiers. Brands licensing content from creators who don't have paid commercial licenses are using unlicensed outputs.

**Your creative team loves AI video. Your legal team won't approve it.**

This is the gap SI8 fills.

## SI8's Solution: Rights Verified Process

SuperImmersive 8 is a **Rights Agency for AI Film**. Every work in our catalog goes through a **Rights Verified vetting process** — a multi-stage legal and technical review that checks:

### What We Verify (Before Catalog Acceptance)

✅ **Commercial Tool Licenses**
- Filmmaker used paid commercial AI tool plans (Runway Pro, Sora Plus, Kling Standard, etc.)
- Receipts on file for each tool used
- Production dates documented

✅ **Human Creative Direction**
- Filmmaker documented meaningful creative control: prompts used, iteration decisions, editorial choices, post-generation editing
- Work qualifies as "AI-assisted, human-directed" (copyrightable standard per US Copyright Office, Taiwan TIPO, Singapore IPOS)
- Not just prompt entry — actual creative authorship

✅ **No Real-Person Likenesses or Voices**
- Only synthetic, fictional faces and voices
- No celebrity lookalikes
- No voice cloning of real individuals
- Hard line, no exceptions

✅ **No Copyrighted IP Imitation**
- No copyrighted characters, brands, or recognizable IP without documented authorization
- Assessed for similarity to protected works

✅ **Platform Compliance**
- Content disclosed as AI-generated/AI-assisted on all platforms
- Meets YouTube, TikTok, Meta, and LinkedIn AI content policies
- C2PA / Content Credentials support where applicable

✅ **Tool Risk Assessment**
- Tools used are on SI8's Approved or Caution tiers (see below)
- No tools with active studio litigation as primary generation source
- No tools that prohibit commercial use (e.g., Veo consumer tier)

### What We Won't Work With

SI8 rejects content that:
- ❌ Uses celebrity or real-person likeness without explicit consent
- ❌ Clones voices of real people without consent
- ❌ Imitates copyrighted characters, brands, or IP without authorization
- ❌ Was produced primarily using tools with active litigation (Minimax/Hailuo, Midjourney for commercial licensing)
- ❌ Was produced on consumer/free tiers that prohibit commercial use
- ❌ Contains political persuasion or deceptive deepfake content

## What You Get: Chain of Title Documentation

Every licensed work from SI8's catalog comes with a **Chain of Title PDF** — structured legal documentation that answers the question: *"Can we defend this if challenged?"*

### The 9-Field Chain of Title Schema

| **Field** | **What It Documents** |
|-----------|----------------------|
| **1. Tool Provenance Log** | Which AI tools, versions, plan types, production dates. Receipts on file. |
| **2. Model Disclosure** | Which AI models generated content (e.g., Runway Gen-3 Alpha, Sora Turbo). Version at time of production. |
| **3. Rights Verified Sign-Off** | SI8 reviewer, review date, tier assigned (Recommended / Standard / Caution), any conditions or flags. |
| **4. Commercial Use Authorization** | Confirmation each tool's terms of service permit commercial licensing. Receipts filed. |
| **5. Modification Rights Status** | Whether filmmaker authorized SI8 to commission brand-integrated regenerations (Tier 2 product placement). Scope: full work or specific scenes. |
| **6. Category Conflict Log** | Brand categories ineligible for placement (e.g., alcohol brands, political campaigns, direct competitors). |
| **7. Territory Log** | Geographic licensing restrictions. Default = Global unless noted. |
| **8. Regeneration Rights Status** | Can specific scenes be AI-regenerated for brand integration? Which scenes? (Tier 2 placement only) |
| **9. Version History** | Production version, review date, any subsequent modifications and re-review dates. |

**Catalog ID format:** Each work gets a unique identifier: `SI8-2026-0001`, `SI8-2026-0002`, etc.

**What this gives you:**
- Documentation ready for client legal/procurement review
- Audit trail if platform policy or regulation requires provenance disclosure
- Defensible position if challenged on IP, likeness, or training data grounds
- Faster creative-to-approval pipeline (3 days vs 3 weeks)
