## Key Pain Points

### 1. **AI Video Quality is Insufficient for Client Work**
**Quote:** *"The only AI video that we can really use is when a client has a high-quality product shot and we just want to use like a simple animation. But as soon as it gets into the realm of any layer of complexity or creating something out of nothing, the quality just starts to decline."*

**Specific issues:**
- Text hallucination (product labels get distorted)
- Motion hallucination (e.g., "open packet" → AI rips packet inside out, does crazy unexpected things)
- Complex movements don't work (hand gestures, product interactions)
- **Result:** "High-quality AI video is not available to us and there's only really specific use cases where it is"

---

### 2. **Premium Brands Can't Use AI-Looking Content**
**Quote:** *"If you're selling a premium product, like we're working with a premium wellness brand, and you're trying to use stuff that looks like AI imagery, that would land quite poorly with the audience."*

**Insight:** Quality threshold is audience-dependent:
- Premium wellness brands → must look photorealistic (high bar)
- Mass market brands → AI look is acceptable (lower bar)
- **The client cares more than the end consumer** ("founders and marketers care about AI look, customers don't")

---

### 3. **Time-Intensive to Produce In-House**
**Quote:** *"We find it really, really time-consuming and intensive and it ends up not actually being a real solution."*

**Current workflow:**
- In-house team attempts AI video with prompts
- Most attempts fail quality threshold
- Only works for simple product animations
- **They want AI video for cost/time savings, but current tools don't deliver**

---

## What Isaac Wants (Buyer Needs)

### Primary Need: **High-Quality AI Video Assets on Demand**
**Quote:** *"My problem is high quality assets on demand at a price that's affordable."*

**Budget:** £1,500 per client (out of £7,500 total 3-month retainer = ~20%)

**Content needs:**
- 30-second to 90-second paid social videos

- Variety needed: product shots, customers using product, lifestyle scenes

### Secondary Need: **Access to Skilled AI Creators**

**Insight:** Isaac is skeptical that photorealistic AI video exists at scale, but **open to being proven wrong** if shown examples.

## Rights Verification Positioning: What Landed

### ❌ **Rights clearance NOT a priority for small agencies**
**Quote:** *"My assumption is that these legal issues affect larger firms rather than small firms like me. So for me, the legality thing, it's interesting. And if it was a feature, then it would make me feel more secure in using your content. But it's not a thing that's going to make me want to buy."*

**Key insight:**
- Legal risk is **"baked in"** assumption (assumes creator handles it)
- Small agencies use unlicensed fonts, images all the time
- Content often lives on ad library for 1 week then disappears (low perceived risk)
- Isaac personally got hit with £75 font licensing claim → knows risk exists but not top of mind

**When it matters:**
- **"For a big, big organization, the legality is like a really important thing."**
- Large brands = legal team demands documentation
- Small agencies = peace of mind, not main driver
