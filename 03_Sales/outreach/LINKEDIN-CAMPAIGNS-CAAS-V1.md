## Handling Key Objections

### "Show me examples"
Previously: catalog blocker. Now: two answers.
> "Here's our showcase page with verified works: superimmersive8.com/showcase. And here's a sample Chain of Title document so you can see exactly what your client's legal team would receive: [link to Neon Dreams PDF]."

### "We handle this internally / our legal team hasn't flagged this yet"
> "Totally — most agencies say the same thing until a brand's procurement team adds it to the brief. When that happens, having a 90-minute turnaround process is useful. Happy to keep the door open."

### "Too expensive / $499 is a lot per video"
> "Makes sense to compare it to the value. A single AI campaign that gets held up by legal review costs multiples of that in delay and revision cycles. The Chain of Title is what removes the blocker — it's the documentation, not legal advice."

### "Are you a real person?" (AI skepticism — learned from March)
> "Ha — yes, real person. [Name] here. Happy to jump on a 5-minute voice note or quick call if it's easier to get a sense of who we are first."
*(Don't argue. Offer a human touchpoint immediately.)*

### "I don't use AI video tools"
> "Totally fair — worth keeping in mind for when clients start requesting it. Brands are moving faster than agencies on this one. Good luck with the work."

## Persona Notes (Standing Encore)

- Keep messages human and slightly informal — "Quick question" not "I am writing to inquire"
- Use first names always: "Hi James" not "Hi James Byrne"
- No em-dashes or corporate language in messages — reads as AI-generated (learned from Starcom "are you a real person?" pushback)
- No exclamation marks in opening lines — feels fake
- One idea per message — don't pack multiple points into a single DM
