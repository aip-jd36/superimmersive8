## Pipeline Stages

### Agency / Brand Buyers
| Stage | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| **Contacted** | Message sent, no reply yet |
| **Replied** | Any response received |
| **Call Booked** | Meeting scheduled |
| **Call Completed** | Discovery call done, notes filed |
| **Proposal Sent** | Formal proposal or follow-up sent |
| **Negotiation** | Active back-and-forth on terms |
| **Won** | Deal closed |
| **Nurture** | Not now, but worth revisiting |
| **Not a Fit** | Wrong role, wrong use case, closed |

### Creators (IG)
| Stage | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| **Contacted** | DM sent, no reply |
| **Replied** | Any response received |
| **Interested** | Actively engaged, asking questions |
| **Comp Sent** | Free Creator Record code sent |
| **Submitted** | Paid or comp submission received |
| **Verified** | Chain of Title issued |
| **Nurture** | Not ready now, warm relationship |
| **Not a Fit** | Wrong profile, disengaged |

## SECTION 3 — ICP Analysis

*Updated as patterns emerge. This is the working analysis layer — not a static summary.*

---

### Buyer ICP (Agency/Brand)

**What we know so far (N=2 calls, ~26 responses):**

**Signals that work:**
- Mid-size agencies (10–50 employees) in London respond to compliance/rights framing
- "Trusted AI Supplier" (Campaign C, white-label framing) outperforms assumption-based openers 2.3x
- London shows higher buying intent per response than Singapore (though Singapore has higher acceptance rates)

**Signals that don't work:**
- Media buying agencies (The Media Shop) are wrong buyer type — they route content to production partners
- Assumptive opener ("Your clients want AI video, but...") creates pushback from London contacts (2 explicit rejections)
- Small agencies (<10 employees, e.g., Isaac) deprioritize rights — quality is buying driver

**Emerging buyer segmentation hypothesis (needs validation):**

| Segment | Primary Pain | Rights Sensitivity | Proof |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|-------|
| Small agencies (<10 employees) | Quality / photorealism | Low | Isaac (N=1) |
| Mid-size agencies (10–50) | Rights / legal risk | High | The Media Shop (N=1) |
| Large agencies / consulting (50+) | Both | Unknown | No data yet |
| Production houses | Quality + rights | Unknown | No data yet |

**ICP hypothesis (current best guess):**
- **Primary buyer:** Creative Director or Head of Production at a mid-size creative/production agency (10–50 employees), London or Singapore, running AI video campaigns for brand clients and facing legal questions from their own clients or legal teams
- **NOT:** Media buying agencies, solo consultants, brand-side marketers without content production responsibility

**Targeting fix needed:** Update LinkedIn filters — remove Media Director/Planner/Strategist; add Creative Director, Head of Production, Producer, Content Director

---

### Creator ICP (Instagram)

**What we know so far (N=7 contacted, 2 substantive conversations):**

**Green flags confirmed:**
- Festival participation = already thinking about provenance and documentation (WAIFF requires tool disclosure)
- Award-winning work = ambition to commercialize, even if not explicit
- Detailed questions = genuine interest; silence = disinterest

**Creator readiness is not about film completion:**
- Shingo has a finished, award-winning film and said "not ready"
- "Ready" = (1) creative confidence in the work + (2) trust in the platform
- Platform trust requires: visible curation philosophy, track record, evidence of quality

**Comp code strategy works:** Free Creator Record removes friction for first engagement. Hot leads emerge from the conversation, not the offer itself.

**Creator ICP hypothesis (current best guess):**
- AI filmmaker with original, finished work
- Active on festival circuit OR has commercial distribution ambition
- Uses paid tool plans (Runway Standard/Pro, Kling Pro, etc.)
- Has an audience (5K+ followers or festival credits) — signals commercial awareness
- NOT hobbyists, reposter accounts, or free-tier-only creators
