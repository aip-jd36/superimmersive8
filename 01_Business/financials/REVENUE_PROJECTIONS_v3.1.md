# Revenue Projections v3.1 — Updated Payout Structure

**Version:** v3.1 (February 2026)
**Business Plan:** BUSINESS_PLAN_v3.md (Rights Agency + AI Product Placement)
**Effective Date:** February 23, 2026
**Model Change:** NEW payout structure (80/20 Tier 1, 50/50 Tier 2)

---

## Executive Summary

**Year 1 Target (6 months):** $52K-$78K SI8 net revenue (vs $16K-$24K under old 20/80 model)

**Key Change:** SI8 retains 80% on Tier 1 catalog licensing (was 20%), splits 50/50 on Tier 2 product placement (was 20%).

**Strategic Rationale:**
- SI8 builds the vetting infrastructure → SI8 captures value from curation layer
- Filmmakers still earn more per deal than self-distribution (platform fees + no buyers)
- Enables SI8 to invest in catalog building, legal infrastructure, platform development
- Aligns with Getty model: value is in the vetting/aggregation, not just content creation

---

## Payout Structure by Tier

| Tier | SI8 Take | Filmmaker Take | Rationale |
|------|----------|----------------|-----------|
| **Tier 1 (Catalog As-Is)** | 80% | 20% | SI8 built the catalog, vetted the content, sourced the buyer. Filmmaker contribution = past production work. |
| **Tier 2 (AI Product Placement)** | 50% | 50% | Filmmaker provides base film + modification rights. SI8 sources brand, manages placement, handles regeneration. Shared value creation. |
| **Tier 3 (Advisory)** | 100% | 0% | Pure SI8 consulting service. Filmmaker not involved. |
| **Tier 4 (Bespoke Production)** | 20% | 80% | Filmmaker does custom work. SI8 is agent/broker. Traditional agency model. |

## Deal Pricing Framework

- Territory (local/regional/global)
- Duration (3 months / 6 months / 12 months / perpetual)
- Usage rights (editorial / streaming / broadcast / commercial)
- Exclusivity (non-exclusive / category-exclusive / full-exclusive)

**Base pricing:**
- Editorial (non-exclusive): $500-$1,500/film

- Airline in-flight (12 months): $1,000-$2,000/film
- Broadcast (regional, 12 months): $3,000-$8,000/film

**Volume discounts:** 3+ films = 15% discount, 10+ films = 25% discount

## Strategic Implications

### 1. Catalog Building is Mission-Critical

**Why:** Tier 1 (80% SI8 take) and Tier 2 (50% SI8 take) both require catalog. More catalog = more licensing opportunities = more passive revenue.

**Goal:** 15-20 Rights Verified catalog entries by Month 3 (before first major pitch)

**Filmmaker pitch:** "Your film earns you passive income every time we license it. You already made it — now it keeps working for you."

---

### 2. Tier 2 is the Highest-Value Product

**Economics:**
- Single Tier 2 deal ($15K-$25K) = SI8 earns $7.5K-$12.5K
- Equivalent to 4-6 Tier 1 deals
- Equivalent to 2-3 Advisory engagements

**Barrier to entry:** Adobe can't offer this (Firefly only, no catalog, no filmmaker relationships)

**Unlock requirement:** Every filmmaker agreement MUST include modification clause before Tier 2 can be sold

---

### 3. Tier 3 Validates the Platform Thesis

**Advisory revenue = proof that buyers care about rights infrastructure**

If brands pay $5K-$15K just for consulting, they'll pay for the platform access in Year 3+.

**Conversion path:** Advisory client → "We should just license from your catalog" → Tier 1/2 deal

---

### 4. Tier 4 is a Safety Valve (Not Primary)

**Purpose:** Handles overflow production demand without SI8 becoming a production shop

**Risk mitigation:** 20% commission keeps SI8 from being production-dependent (the trap Layer 1 is meant to avoid)

**Friday Rule still applies:** Don't take production work that blocks catalog building or buyer development

## Risk Factors & Mitigation

### Risk 1: Filmmakers reject 20% on Tier 1

**Mitigation:**
- Frame as passive income they wouldn't have otherwise
- Show total earnings potential (Tier 1 + Tier 2 combined)
- Lead with Tier 2 (50/50) as primary pitch, Tier 1 as bonus upside
- Emphasize: "We built the catalog, we source the buyers, we handle legal — you already made the film"

---

### Risk 2: Not enough Tier 2 deals (50/50 is great, but only if deals happen)

**Mitigation:**
- Build catalog first (15-20 entries by Month 3)
- Modification clause in every filmmaker agreement from Day 1
- Target 2-4 Tier 2 deals in first 6 months (conservative)
- Even 1 Tier 2 deal = $7.5K-$12.5K SI8 revenue

---

### Risk 3: Tier 1 volume too low (need multiple deals to hit target)

**Mitigation:**
- MyVideo pilot = potential 5-10 film catalog license (could be $10K+ gross in one deal)
- Airlines, streaming platforms = multi-film buyers (not one-offs)
- Volume discounts incentivize bulk licensing
- Goal: 4-6 Tier 1 deals in 6 months (achievable with 15-20 catalog entries)

---

### Risk 4: Advisory deals depend on reputation (no case studies yet)

**Mitigation:**
- Advisory board (3-5 people) = instant credibility
- Rights Verified documentation project = public IP (demonstrates expertise)
- LinkedIn content (build in public) = thought leadership
- Event (Month 3) = lead generation engine
- First advisory client likely comes from warm network, not cold outreach
