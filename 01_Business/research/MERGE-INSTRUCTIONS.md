## Merge Strategy

### Step 1: Identify Overlapping Findings
- All three AIs will likely find the same major cases (Thomson Reuters, Andersen, NYT v. OpenAI)
- **Action:** Consolidate duplicates, keeping the most detailed version
- **Add:** "Confirmed by multiple sources" notation

### Step 2: Identify Unique Findings
- Each AI may find different cases, regulations, or precedents
- **Action:** Add all unique findings to respective sections
- **Flag:** "Source: ChatGPT" or "Source: Gemini" for attribution

### Step 3: Cross-Validate Quantifiable Claims
- Example: "73% damage reduction" (innocent infringer defense)
- **Action:** If all three AIs confirm same statistic, mark as "Triple-Verified"
- **If discrepancy:** Flag for manual verification

### Step 4: Resolve Contradictions
- Example: One AI says "documentation helps," another says "documentation doesn't help"
- **Action:** Note the contradiction, provide context for both views
- **Resolution:** Usually both are right in different contexts (helps for damages, doesn't prevent liability)

### Step 5: Compile Direct Quotes
- Extract all quotable language from all three sources
- **Create:** "Quotes Library" section organized by topic
- **Include:** Full attribution and links for each quote

## Quality Checks

### Verification Tiers

**Tier 1: Triple-Verified (Highest Confidence)**
- All three AIs found same information
- Multiple independent sources cited
- Statutory or case law (not just opinion)
- Mark with ✅✅✅

**Tier 2: Dual-Verified (High Confidence)**
- Two of three AIs found it
- At least one authoritative source
- Mark with ✅✅

**Tier 3: Single-Source (Moderate Confidence)**
- Only one AI found it
- Source credibility matters (court > blog)
- Mark with ✅

**Tier 4: Unverified (Needs Further Research)**
- Contradictory information
- Weak sourcing
- Mark with ⚠️

### Citation Standards

**Court Cases:**
- Full case name
- Court and docket number
- Date of decision
- Link to opinion or reliable source

**Statutes:**
- Full citation (e.g., 17 U.S.C. § 504(c)(2))
- Link to Cornell LII or official source

**Regulatory Guidance:**
- Agency name and document title
- Date issued
- Link to official .gov source

**Expert Opinions:**
- Name, credentials, organization
- Publication or statement source
- Date
- Link
