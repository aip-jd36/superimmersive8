# Pivot Validation: Showcase + Rights Verified Platform

**Date:** March 2026
**Status:** Pre-PRD validation
**Version:** Proposed v4 direction

---

## The Proposed Pivot

**From:** Rights-only agency model (v3 - demand-side first, catalog licensing + AI product placement)

**To:** Two-tier platform:
- **Showcase (free):** Portfolio hosting for ALL AI video creators - even copyrighted/fan art content allowed
- **Rights Verified (premium):** Vetted, licensable content for commercial use - existing Safe Lane process

**Key changes:**
1. Free portfolio hosting for creators (removes barrier to entry)
2. Allow copyrighted content on Showcase (Marvel fan art, Nike-style work) - portfolio purposes only
3. Rights Verified becomes optional premium feature (not required to list on platform)
4. 50% commission for Tier 1 licensing (not 80%)
5. Self-serve platform (not just agency services)

## What Problems Are We Trying to Solve?

### Problem 1: Chicken-Egg (From Essa & Leon Calls)

**Evidence:**
- **Essa** (Hong Kong filmmaker): Asked detailed economics questions, wanted proof MyVideo deal is real, went silent after JD's follow-up
- **Leon** (LKDN_OFFICIAL): Responded with just "Hi", ignored 3 follow-ups, never asked any questions
- **Pattern:** Both responded to initial pitch, neither converted to submission

**Root cause identified:**
> "Filmmakers want proof of buyers before submitting; buyers want catalog before committing."

**Does Showcase pivot solve this?**
- ✅ **YES** - Creators can join NOW for free portfolio hosting (immediate value regardless of buyers)
- ✅ **YES** - Builds catalog without needing buyers proven first
- ⚠️ **PARTIAL** - Still need to attract creators to Showcase (but lower barrier than Rights Verified)

---

### Problem 2: Commission Split Feels Steep (From Essa Call)

**Evidence:**
- Essa asked: "what percentage payment are we receiving from your sales"
- JD explained: "We take 80% for finding the clients, handling the curation and representation, and the rights vetting."
- Essa asked follow-up: "how much is cost for client in total ?? At what price would be the 80%/20% ??"
- JD revealed: "$2K-$5K as a total revenue" → Essa would get $400-$1,000
- **Result:** Essa went silent after this conversation

**Our research found:** Getty/Shutterstock pay 15-20% to contributors (platform keeps 80-85%), so 80/20 IS industry standard

**BUT:** SI8 hasn't earned Getty-level commission without proven distribution network

**Does 50% commission solve this?**
- ✅ **YES** - More defensible split when platform is unproven
- ✅ **YES** - Easier to recruit creators ("fair 50/50 split" vs "we take 80%")
- ❌ **NO** - Still performance-based (no upfront fee), which Essa wanted

**Essa's stated preference:**
> "I can release the rights of my works for a purchased fee for further sales on your platform, per video"

This pivot doesn't address Essa's actual ask (upfront purchase), but makes commission feel fairer.

### Problem 3: Performance-Based Risk (From Essa & Leon Calls)

**Evidence:**
- **Essa:** Wanted upfront fee per video (guaranteed income)

- **Both:** No guaranteed income = risk for filmmaker

- ⚠️ **PARTIAL** - Licensing still performance-based, but now creators get portfolio value upfront

## What This Pivot Enables

### ✅ Solves: Catalog Building Without Buyers

**Before (v3):** Need buyers committed to build catalog → Essa/Leon won't submit without buyers proven → Stalemate

**After (Showcase):** Offer free portfolio hosting → Creators join for exposure → Catalog builds organically → Buyers discover content → Some creators upgrade to Rights Verified → Licensing deals close

**Freemium conversion funnel:**
1. 100 creators join Showcase (free portfolio)
2. 10,000 monthly platform visitors (buyers + other creators)
3. 10 buyers request licensing for specific work
4. 30 creators see licensing demand → submit for Rights Verified
5. 5 licensing deals close ($10K total GMV)
6. Success stories attract next 100 creators

---

### ✅ Solves: "Why Should I Join?" (Value Prop)

**Before (v3):** "Submit your work for Rights Verified vetting. If buyers license it, you get 20% royalty."
- **Value:** Only get paid if buyer licenses (performance risk)
- **Barrier:** Need receipts, documentation, time investment (~20 min submission)
- **Result:** Essa/Leon won't commit without proof

**After (Showcase):** "Get your AI video work in front of programmers, ad buyers, and brands. Free portfolio hosting. If you want to enable licensing, upgrade to Rights Verified."
- **Value:** Free exposure NOW (regardless of licensing)
- **Barrier:** Just paste YouTube link, add title/description (2-min submission)
- **Result:** Lower barrier → more creators join

---

### ✅ Enables: Portfolio Content That Can't Be Licensed

**Before (v3):** Only accept Rights Verified content (no copyrighted elements allowed)
- **Problem:** Eliminates most impressive creator work (Marvel fan films, Nike-style commercials, music video treatments)
- **Problem:** Creators use copyrighted content to showcase skills, but can't submit if it won't pass Rights Verified

**After (Showcase):** Allow copyrighted portfolio work on Showcase (NOT licensable)
- **Benefit:** Creators can show best work (even if not commercially cleared)
- **Benefit:** Buyers see creator's actual skill level and style
- **Benefit:** More attractive catalog for discovery/browsing

**Real use case:** Creator makes Nike-style AI commercial (not for Nike, just portfolio). Can't license it (Nike trademark), BUT shows they can create high-end commercial work. Buyer sees it, reaches out for custom brief (Tier 4).

---

### ⚠️ Creates: Two-Product Complexity

**New risk:** Buyers might get confused about what's licensable vs. portfolio-only

**Mitigation strategies:**
1. Clear visual distinction (Rights Verified checkmark badge on approved content)
2. Buyer-facing default view = Rights Verified only (toggle to "Show All")
3. "Request License" button only appears on Rights Verified content
4. Legal disclaimer: "Showcase content is portfolio work, not pre-cleared for licensing"

**Similar precedent:** Behance (portfolio showcase) vs. Adobe Stock (licensing) - separate but linked

**Key difference:** SI8 combines both in one platform (Showcase + Rights Verified Catalog)

## What This Pivot Does NOT Solve

### ❌ Still Need Traffic to Platform

**Problem:** Building Showcase catalog doesn't automatically bring buyers
- Need marketing, SEO, outreach to drive buyer traffic
- Creators won't see licensing value if no buyers visit platform

**v3 approach:** Manual outreach to MyVideo, SEA agencies (JD finding buyers)
**Showcase approach:** Still need outreach OR content marketing OR events to drive traffic

**This hasn't changed** - still need distribution strategy for buyers

---

### ❌ Still Need Creator Recruitment

**Problem:** Free portfolio hosting is better value prop, but still need to recruit creators
- Outreach effort required (Instagram DMs, AI filmmaker communities)
- Essa/Leon didn't convert even with better messaging - pattern might continue

**Lower barrier ≠ automatic adoption**

**Required:** Marketing/outreach to creator community (unchanged from v3)

---

### ❌ Content Moderation Burden

**New problem:** Allowing copyrighted Showcase content creates DMCA risk
- If Marvel fan video gets popular, Marvel could send DMCA takedown
- Need DMCA agent registration ($6)
- Need takedown response process
- Legal disclaimer required

**Time cost:** Reviewing Showcase submissions (even light moderation) adds founder time

---

### ❌ Two-Tier Confusion Risk

**New problem:** Buyers might not understand Showcase (portfolio) vs. Rights Verified (licensable)
- "I want to license this Marvel video" → "Sorry, that's portfolio only, not cleared for licensing"
- Buyer frustration if not clearly communicated

**Mitigation required:** UX design, clear labeling, buyer education

## Competitive Landscape Check

### Who Else Does This?

**Portfolio platforms (no licensing):**
- **Behance:** Design/creative portfolios, no licensing, acquired by Adobe 2012
- **Dribbble:** Design portfolios, no licensing
- **Vimeo:** Video portfolios, some licensing (Vimeo Stock), but not primary model

**Licensing platforms (no portfolio showcase):**
- **Adobe Stock:** Stock video licensing, contributors can't show copyrighted work
- **Shutterstock:** Stock video licensing, strict content policy
- **Getty Images:** Curated licensing, no free portfolio tier

**AI-specific platforms:**
- **Escape.ai:** AI creator platform, allows showcase + monetization (direct competitor?)
- **Curious Refuge:** Education + production studio, building creator network
- **Runway AIFF:** Festival (annual), not year-round platform

**SI8's differentiation if we do Showcase + Rights Verified:**
- Only platform combining free portfolio showcase (with copyrighted content allowed) + Rights Verified licensing tier
- Only platform with Safe Lane vetting process (legal defensibility focus)
- Only platform focused on APAC + Europe markets (geographic gap)

**Direct threat:** Escape.ai (if they add rights vetting layer)

## Revenue Model Implications

### Before (v3): Agency Services + Licensing Commissions

**Year 1 revenue target:** $80-120K
- Production (Layer 1): $50-70K (agency retainers + sprints)
- Distribution commissions: $5-10K (25% of deals brokered)
- Event sponsorships: $10-20K
- Consulting: $15-30K

**Assumed:** Catalog licensing commissions = small part of revenue (most from production services)

---

### After (Showcase): Platform + Licensing Commissions

**New revenue model:**
- **Showcase:** Free for creators (no revenue)
- **Rights Verified submission:** Free or $100-300 review fee? (TBD)
- **Licensing commissions:** 50% of deal value (not 80%)

**Impact on Year 1 revenue:**

**Positive:**
- More creators → larger catalog → more buyer interest
- Showcase attracts traffic → event/consulting leads

**Negative:**
- 50% commission (not 80%) = need MORE deals to hit same revenue
- Free Showcase = no submission fees
- Platform development time = less time for production services (Layer 1)

**Break-even math:**

| Model | Commission | Deals Needed for $10K Revenue |
|-------|-----------|------------------------------|
| **v3 (80%)** | $4,000 per $5K deal | 3 deals |
| **Showcase (50%)** | $2,500 per $5K deal | 4 deals |

**Need 33% more deals** to hit same licensing revenue.

**BUT:** Larger catalog (from Showcase) might generate more deal volume → could offset lower commission percentage.

**Unknown:** Will Showcase actually drive more licensing deals, or just more portfolio views?

---

### Cash Flow Impact

**v3 plan:** Focus on production services (Layer 1) for cash flow, distribution as side project

**Showcase plan:** Platform development requires:
- 6-8 weeks founder time for MVP build
- Ongoing: content moderation, creator support, Rights Verified reviews
- **Less time for production services** → risk to Layer 1 revenue

**Founder time reallocation:**

| Activity | v3 (10-15 hrs/week) | Showcase (15-25 hrs/week) |
|----------|---------------------|--------------------------|
| Revenue outreach + calls | 5 hrs | 3 hrs (less time available) |
| Delivery system + creators | 3 hrs | 2 hrs |
| Website + assets | 2 hrs | 0 hrs (MVP built) |
| Platform development | 0 hrs | 8-10 hrs (MVP phase) |
| Content moderation | 0 hrs | 2 hrs (Showcase reviews) |
| Rights Verified reviews | 1 hr | 2-3 hrs (more submissions) |

**Risk:** Platform build reduces time for production services → Layer 1 revenue drops

**Mitigation:** Delay platform build until Layer 1 revenue is stable? Or accept lower Year 1 revenue as platform investment?
