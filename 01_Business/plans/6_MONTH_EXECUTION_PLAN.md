### Legal & Rights Infrastructure (Core Competency)

**Why this matters:**
SI8's positioning is "the trusted operator" who helps creators and brands navigate AI video **safely and legally**. This requires deep understanding of the evolving AI content legal landscape ‚Äî not just contracts, but the underlying rights issues.

**The moat:** Most agencies and filmmakers are confused about AI rights. If SI8 becomes the expert who can answer "Is this legal? Is this brand-safe? What rights do we need?" ‚Äî that's a defensible competitive advantage.

**Key areas to master:**

| Area | Why It Matters | Who Cares |
|------|----------------|-----------|
| **AI Copyright (by jurisdiction)** | Can AI-generated content be copyrighted? Who owns it? | Filmmakers, platforms |
| **Training Data Rights** | Was the AI model trained legally? Liability exposure? | Agencies, brands |
| **Likeness & Voice Rights** | Using real people's faces/voices in AI content | Brands, agencies |
| **Platform Policies** | What do streaming platforms require/prohibit for AI content? | Distribution deals |
| **Disclosure Requirements** | When must content be labeled as AI-generated? | Brands, platforms |
| **Music & Audio Rights** | AI-generated music, sound effects, voice cloning | All productions |
| **Brand Safety** | What makes AI content "safe" for brand association? | Agencies, brands |
| **International Differences** | US vs EU vs APAC regulations | Cross-border deals |

**Deliverable by Month 6:** "AI Video Rights Playbook" ‚Äî internal document (potentially productizable) that covers:
- Current legal landscape summary
- Rights checklist for productions
- Platform-by-platform requirements
- Risk assessment framework
- Standard contract clauses

**This becomes a sales asset:**
> "We've done the legal homework so you don't have to. Here's our rights clearance process."

*These break first in 90% of services + brand businesses:*

**üî• #1: Production scope creep**
- Client keeps "just one more tweak" ‚Üí you lose 3 weeks ‚Üí Layer 2 dies
- **Fix:** Enforce package constraints + revision limits strictly

**üî• #2: Too many workstreams (context switching)**
- Start 6 initiatives, finish none
- **Fix:** Stable weekly operating cadence. Same activities, same days.

**üî• #3: Event becomes a time sink**

- **Fix:** Outsource event operations early. Venue + drinks + ticketing = contractor. You only own: speakers, content, conversion.

**üî• #5: MyVideo stalls and blocks everything**

### Key Legal Questions to Answer

**Copyright & Ownership:**
- Can AI-generated content be copyrighted in US? Taiwan? Singapore?
- Who owns the output: prompter, tool company, or no one?
- How do different AI tools (Runway, Midjourney, Sora) handle IP?

**Training Data & Liability:**
- What lawsuits are pending against AI video tools?
- What liability exposure do users of these tools have?
- How to assess "clean" vs "risky" AI tools?

**Likeness & Voice:**
- When can AI-generated faces be used commercially?
- What are the rules for voice cloning?
- Celebrity likeness vs. synthetic faces?

**Platform Requirements:**
- What do streaming platforms require for AI content disclosure?
- What content restrictions exist (violence, adult, political)?
- How do ad platforms (Meta, Google) treat AI content?

**Brand Safety:**
- What makes AI content "brand-safe"?
- What disclosures do brands need?
- How to document provenance and rights chain?
