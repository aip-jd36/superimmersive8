Rights Verified Vetting Criteria

Pass/fail thresholds for each of the seven review categories. Clear standards remove ambiguity where judgment isn't needed — human expertise reserved for genuine gray areas.

01

Tool & Plan Verification

Every AI tool used has a paid commercial license and is on the Approved or Caution tier list.

✓ Pass

  • All tools on Approved or Caution tier list
  • Paid plan receipts on file for every tool
  • Commercial license status confirmed

✗ Fail

  • Prohibited or High-Risk tool as primary source (Hailuo, non-commercial plans)
  • Missing receipts for any tool

⚠ Flag

  • Caution-tier tool (Kling) as primary → note in Chain of Title; affects exclusivity pricing
  • Tool version unclear → affects traceability
02

Human Authorship Evidence Quality

Declaration demonstrates meaningful human creative direction — sufficient to establish human authorship, not just prompt entry.

✓ Pass

  • Describes specific iteration decisions (what tried, kept, why)
  • Describes editorial choices (pacing, structure, scene selection)
  • Describes post-generation editing if occurred
  • Minimum 150 words — substantive, not padding

✗ Fail

  • Generic: "I wrote prompts and selected outputs" with no specifics
  • AI made all meaningful creative decisions
  • Declaration contradicts work itself

⚠ Conditional

  • Substantive but brief — request expansion
  • Some categories described well, others missing
Why it matters: US Copyright Office (Jan 2025) requires meaningful human authorship for copyright registration. Taiwan and Singapore follow similar principles. Strong declaration protects the filmmaker, not just SI8.
03

Likeness & Identity

No identifiable real person's face, voice, or identity without documented consent.

✓ Pass

  • All faces clearly synthetic and non-identifiable
  • All voices original or AI-generated with no specific real-person identity
  • No character clearly evokes a specific real person

✗ Fail

  • Any identifiable real-person face without consent
  • Any voice clone/imitation of specific real person without consent
  • Any character clearly representing a real person
  • Any doubt on any of the above
Hard line: No exceptions. No gray area. If a face is possibly identifiable, it fails. This is the highest legal exposure category. "Probably fine" is not a pass.
Lookalike test: If published, would any reasonable viewer say "that looks like [specific real person]"? If yes → fail.
04

IP & Brand Imitation

No copyrighted characters, protected trade dress, or trademarked identifiers without authorization.

✓ Pass

  • No character/creature/logo/brand element recognized as belonging to real IP holder
  • No replication/parody of real brand trade dress
  • No trademarked slogans, logos, or identifiers

✗ Fail

  • Clear imitation of copyrighted character
  • Protected trade dress replicated (causes consumer confusion)
  • Trademarked logos/identifiers visible and identifiable

⚠ Gray Area

  • Ambiguous similarity — shares some features but not clearly the same
  • Stylistic reference to brands but not direct imitation
  • Parody that may/may not qualify for fair use (requires legal review)
Standard: Would a reasonable IP holder's lawyer send a cease-and-desist? If yes → fail. If unsure → gray area.
05

Brand Safety

Content suitability for commercial brand attachment, and which brand categories are appropriate.

✓ Pass

  • Suitable for mainstream commercial brand attachment, no restrictions
  • OR has specific documented restrictions in Chain of Title category conflict log

⚠ Conditional (suitable for some, not all)

  • Mature themes but no explicit content → not for children's/family/certain regulated categories
  • Contains alcohol, gambling, political content, competitors → document restriction precisely

✗ Fail

  • Graphic violence (realistic or prolonged)
  • Sexual content (explicit or strongly implied)
  • Political persuasion or propaganda
  • Drug use (celebratory or instructional)
  • Any content on the No List
Test question: "Would a Fortune 500 brand's marketing team approve this for their brand?" Yes → Pass. Yes-with-restrictions → Conditional and document. No → Fail.
06

Audio & Music Rights

Every audio element is original AI-generated with commercial license, licensed with documentation, or absent.

✓ Pass

  • All audio is original AI-generated using paid commercial plans (receipts on file)
  • All licensed audio has documentation: license type, licensor, file, coverage scope
  • Work is silent (no audio)

✗ Fail

  • Unlicensed commercial music without documentation
  • "Licensed" claimed but no documentation provided
  • Audio license doesn't cover commercial use or specific territory/medium

⚠ Conditional (flag for legal review)

  • Licensed music with unusual terms ("personal use," unclear sync license)
  • AI-generated music from tool with unclear commercial license scope
  • "Royalty-free" that may not cover commercial distribution
Important: "Royalty-free" ≠ "free of all licensing obligations." It means no per-use royalties. Many royalty-free licenses still restrict commercial use, AI integration, or distribution channels.
07

Modification Rights (Tier 2 Gate)

Filmmaker authorized SI8 to commission AI-regenerated brand-integrated versions for Tier 2 product placement deals.

✓ Authorized — Full Work

  • Tier 2 eligible (all scenes)
  • Note: Shopping Agreement must include modification clause; if missing, Tier 2 cannot be offered until amended

⚠ Authorized — Specific Scenes

  • Tier 2 eligible for specified scenes only
  • Document scene list precisely in Chain of Title

✗ Not Authorized

  • Tier 1 only — note clearly in Chain of Title
  • Filmmaker can update authorization anytime (requires Chain of Title update and re-review of this category only)
Scope note: Modification authorization affects which Tier 2 products can be offered. Full authorization = maximum flexibility. Scene-specific = limited to those scenes.

The No List

Hard boundaries that protect filmmakers, clients, and SI8 from highest-risk areas:

✗ Celebrity Likeness

Real people's faces without consent

✗ Voice Cloning

Real person voices without authorization

✗ IP Imitation

Copyrighted characters, brands, trademarks

✗ Deepfakes

Deceptive content pretending to be real

✗ Political Content

Persuasion or advocacy content

✗ CMI Stripping

Watermark/attribution removal (DMCA violation)

✗ Prohibited Tools

Veo consumer tier, tools in active litigation

✗ No Human Authorship

Prompts alone (not copyrightable per USCO)
